Lab 4: Pseudocoding and Problem-solving

Challenge

For this lab, the issue we were presented was to break down a complex task or goal into more manageable steps, and present that as code.

Problems

One of the easiest things to do when doing this assignment was to overcomplicate or overthink things. Whether it be everything that required getting an A in the class entailed or how each chess piece moves.

Results

After process of elimination, and reigning in some of our overthought steps, we managed to create concise and organized psuedocode for our tasks.

Getting an A in a class

  1. Preview before every lecture
  2. Participate in every lecture
  3. Review after every lecture
  4. Turn in assignments/projects on time
  5. Go to office hours
  6. Try to teach other students that are confused in your way of understanding

Game of Chess

  1. Print the board
  2. Accept player role choice
  3. Initiate Player turn
  4. Test for "check" condition
  5. Accept Player input
  6. Test for "Checkmate" Condition
